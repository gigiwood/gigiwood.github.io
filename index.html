Iowa Media Tips

Despite the hype, the press release is anything but dead. After spending more than 15 years in newsrooms across Iowa, I've learned what it takes to get your story published in the newspaper or broadcast on radio and TV.

A story published in the local newspaper can have a powerful effect on your business. That story can be "picked up" by other media outlets, including other regional newspapers, as well as radio and TV stations.

That said, it is important to follow the rules when it comes to pitching your story and submitting a press release to a news team. News organizations receive hundreds of story pitches and press releases each day. And for the most part, each one is read and reviewed, because news teams are always on the lookout for the next great story.

Journalists are working on tight deadlines. As editors and reporters balance the review of story pitches with covering stories, writing stories, editing stories and layout of those stories on a page, nothing can be more frustrating than someone who follows up the submission of a press release with multiple phone calls.
